I am a soon-to-be computer science PhD student at Columbia University; I received an NSF Fellowship to work on building better models of language using poetry and creative writing.

Which is my second thing: I'm a writer and a poet. I regularly blog for SheCanCode. I've most recently been published by Synaesthesia Magazine, the Harvard Bookstore, and Spare Change News.

I've always dabbled in the visual arts. In the past I've experimented with film photography and been obsessed with charcoal portraits and figure drawing; I've recently gotten into painting. You can find my work on Flickr and some work I'm most recently proud of below.

I did my undergraduate degree in mechanical engineering which means I've built physical things for the real world. I've blogged about a variety of projects since my sophomore year on wordpress including some art installations I built on-campus. Some of my favorite projects are also below.
