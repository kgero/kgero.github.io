I am a soon-to-be computer science PhD student at Columbia University. I have received an NSF Fellowship to work on building better computationl models of language using poetry and creative writing.

Which is my second thing: I'm a writer and a poet. I've most recently been published by Synaesthesia Magazine, the Harvard Bookstore, and Spare Change News. I regularly blog about women in tech for SheCanCode.

I've always dabbled in the visual arts. In the past I've experimented with film photography and been obsessed with charcoal portraits and figure drawing; I've recently gotten into painting. You can find my work on Flickr and some work I'm most recently proud of below.

I did my undergraduate degree in mechanical engineering which means I've built physical things for the real world. I've blogged about a variety of projects since my sophomore year, including some art installations I built on campus and for some reason many lamps.

Nude Mural

I painted this mural inside of Senior House, a dormitory at MIT, during my last semester as an undergraduate. It is based on a photograph I took as part of a nude photography class offered at the MIT Student Art Association. The mural is located in a hallway, which means you can only ever see it up close. Up close it is larger than life and somewhat abstract.

This photo was taken as part of a mural documentation and preservation project. You can find more murals from the dormitory here.
